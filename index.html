Let's get you on the right track to your fitness goals.

GAINS

     To most people's surprise it is actually quite the process in order to have lasting, healthy gains. It is recommended increasing your caloric intake and also increasing the amount you're lifting in the gym in order to start to see those changes. Like losing weight, you have to consider your caloric intake to your caloric output but to gain, you would need to be consuming more than you are burning.

     Whether you are active in the gym or simply inactive, you still have to take in account that you burn a minimal amount of calories daily. Therefor, you would still need to consume over the minimum amount of calories needed to survive throughout the day in order to see gains. TRIMMING

     A more common goal of individuals getting into their fitness journeys would be to lose weight, or as well call it, trim down! With the right mindset and determination, one can do so by simply eating right and working out with light to moderate weights and cardio. Trimming down body fat means taking in fewer calories than what you are burning off on a daily basis, whether you are extremely, mildly or barely active.

     Healthy weight loss is strategic and a process. One wouldn’t want to deprive themselves of basic needs so, they would aim for a balance of proper nutrition and exercise. A helpful method to find out how many calories you need to consume daily based on your physical activity would be the Harris Bennedict Equation. Click here for suggested workouts and recipes to aid in Trimming down.

MAINTENANCE

     Maintaining down to the core is not easy, as people's day-to-day, week-to-week and even month-to-month activities can vary and often change. In order to maintain, balance is key! Balance of caloric output to caloric intake can be done so by tracking your activities and practicing healthy eating and exercise habits. Overall, the ratio should be the same.

     For anyone starting or following their fitness journey, one should take into account the nutritional value of the calories you are consuming. For example, a calorie is simply put, a measure of energy. Each gram of protein contains 4 calories, each gram of carbohydrate contains 4 and each gram of fat contains 9 calories. Mindful eating & a little old school calorie counting can be useful when aiming to maintain. See suggested workouts and recipes for maintenance here.
